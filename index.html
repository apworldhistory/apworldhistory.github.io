The Five Pillars of Islam

The Five Pillars consist of: Shahadah: sincerely reciting the Muslim profession of faith. Salat: performing ritual prayers in the proper way five times each day. Zakat: paying an alms (or charity) tax to benefit the poor and the needy. Siyam: Fasting from dawn to sunset during the month of Ramadan. Hajj: making the journey to Makkah during the prilgramige season.

Shahadah

Shahadah means "There is no god but God, and Muhammad is the messenger of God." It reminds us that there is only one Creator, who sent messengers and revealed words of guidance to humans. Individually, each Muslim has a relationship with God.

Salat

Salat meaning is to perform the five daily prairs as Muhammad did. Spiritually it means to obey God's command to worship. In a community, it binds people together in regular worship and contact.

Zakah

Zakah is not an option, it is a compulsory act for all Muslims. It is a contribution paid once a year on savings of two and a half percent. This giving is to “cleanse” your money and possessions from excessive desire for them or greed. The idea is, that by giving this money you learn not to place too much importance on material wealth.

Siyam

Siyam, the fourth pillar of the muslim religion, is celebrated in the month of Ramadan. Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar when muslims fast during all of its 30 days.

Hajj

The Hajj is an annual Islamic pilgrimage to Mecca, the holiest city for Muslims, and a mandatory religious duty for Muslims that must be carried out at least once in their lifetime.

The Hajj Today

The Hajj is still completed today. Just this year, more than two million Muslims gathered to complete it. The Hajj is still an important part of the Muslim tradition.
